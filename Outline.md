# Witnessed Receptivity — A synthesis of trauma, sexuality, myth, and culture

Author: Pekka-Ilmari Nikander, with ChatGPT 5.2

## Abstract (placeholder draft, to be rewritten later)

Even relative absence of the _witnessing other_ (the mother)
has long-term consequences for the development of a human being.
Yet, when the mother (or other) is physically present,
if the baby doesn't feel being "seen" as a separate entity,
the experience, when prolonged, may lead to deep trauma.
In this essay (work in progress), we argue that quite a few
people in Western culture may have such trauma, at least in a mild sense,
creating a self-reinforcing structure where
mild trauma-like psychological structures stabilise our culture in one hand and
the culture contributes to inter-generational stability of such structures in the other hand.
In essence, we pave road towards a structural claim that
certain political-symbolic orders and certain trauma-like organisations stabilise each other.

## Disclaimer

This essay has been written with essential help from ChatGPT 5.2.
Without ChatGPT, I would not have been able to some of ground the work to the literature.
Hence, it is certainly possible that some of the details in this essay are
potentially confabulated by the LLM and therefore may be not so well founded
and grounded within the literature than I would like them to be.
ChatGPT was also helpful with the analysis and expression, and
with identifying supportive theories and myths that were not familiar to me
when I started this work.

However, the overall argument and the presented structural analysis itself
is a result of human insight and synthesis. Furthermore, I have reviewed and revised
all the details, but still errors may and are even likely to remain, in this version.

My aim is to make later versions of this essay more humanly grounded.
At this point the reader is advised to take a critical position on the details,
hopefully without allowing any faults in the details to necessarily collapse the whole structure.
With gradual weeding of eventual faults and plugging of remaining holes I believe that
my central observations and claims will hold.

With these caveats, I hope that this essay brings forth the identified
structural issue, a largely hidden mutually reinforcing loop between trauma and culture.
